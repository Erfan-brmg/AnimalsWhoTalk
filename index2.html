ANIMALS WHO TALK

Ever since I was a little girl I’ve loved animal stories. The Elephant’s Child, Eye-Ore, Reepicheep and The Muddle-Headed Wombat are lifelong friends.

My passion for animal characters never waned. And so it wasn’t surprising that my doctoral research explored anthropomorphism and animal characters in Australian children’s literature

Since the publication of The Shark Caller and The Dog with Seven Names plus my PHD, my interest in all things anthropomorphic continues to grow.

This site is home to information and blog posts about diverse animal voices.

Humans are not the only ones who talk.

ABOUT DIANNE WOLFER

Dianne is the award-winning author of 19 books for children and teenagers.

Her PhD research into the Crafting of Animal Characters in Australian Children’s Literature resulted in two novels, The Dog with Seven Names and The Shark Caller. The latter, a cross-cultural quest novel, was a CBCA Notable and shortlisted for WAYRBA. The former, also a CBCA Notable, was shortlisted for the NSW Premier’s Awards, Speech Pathology Awards and will be published in China.
